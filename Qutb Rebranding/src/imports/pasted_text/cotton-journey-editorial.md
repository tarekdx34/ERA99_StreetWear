QUTB — Cotton Journey Page (Editorial Luxury Prompt)
You are a world-class fashion creative director, UX designer, and frontend engineer.
Design and build a “Cotton Journey” editorial experience page for the brand QUTB.
This is not a technical material breakdown. It is a soft luxury narrative about fabric, touch, and lifestyle elevation.
________________________________________
Brand Direction (critical)
QUTB positioning:
•	Premium Egyptian basics label 
•	Inspired by TOTEME minimalism, Jacquemus softness, Aimé Leon Dore warmth 
•	Calm luxury, airy editorial space 
•	Less industrial, more human and tactile 
•	Fabric is emotional, not technical 
Visual language:
•	Off-white / bone dominant background 
•	Soft charcoal text 
•	Muted sand, warm beige, faded black accents 
•	Gentle contrast, no harsh blacks unless typography anchor 
•	Typography: refined serif or elegant grotesk pairing (editorial fashion magazine feel) 
Tone:
•	Quiet, poetic, observational 
•	Avoid engineering language 
•	No GSM charts, no lab framing 
•	Focus on feeling, movement, wear, memory 
________________________________________
Page Concept
Title: “A Journey in Cotton”
Subtitle:
“What begins as fiber becomes the rhythm of daily wear.”
This page should feel like:
•	A fashion editorial spread 
•	A museum textile exhibit softened into lifestyle 
•	A Jacquemus runway story translated into web form 
________________________________________
Page Structure
1. Hero — Editorial Silence
•	Full screen off-white background 
•	Large serif headline:
“A JOURNEY IN COTTON” 
•	Subline in small spaced caps:
“FROM FIBER TO FORM” 
Behavior:
•	Slow fade-in 
•	Subtle grain paper texture 
•	Almost no motion 
Mood reference: TOTEME editorial homepage energy
________________________________________
2. Origin — The Field
Describe cotton at origin as a natural moment.
Content:
•	Cotton as a living crop 
•	Sunlight, soil, seasonal rhythm 
•	Egyptian cotton heritage implied subtly, not technically explained 
Layout:
•	Large imagery-first layout 
•	Full-bleed soft photography (fields, hands, fabric in light) 
•	Minimal text overlays 
Tone:
•	“Cotton grows slowly, under light that shapes its patience.” 
________________________________________
3. The Fiber — Soft Structure
Explain fiber as texture and possibility.
Focus:
•	Length = softness and endurance (no technical detail tables) 
•	Natural irregularity as beauty 
•	Fiber as “beginning of touch” 
UI:
•	Macro photography with blurred depth 
•	Floating captions, not blocks of text 
________________________________________
4. The Thread — Becoming Fabric
Transition from fiber to yarn and cloth.
Focus:
•	Continuity, weaving as quiet repetition 
•	Craft over machinery 
•	Fabric as daily companion 
Design:
•	Slow horizontal scroll section (optional) 
•	Overlapping text and fabric motion shots 
•	Aimé Leon Dore editorial layering style 
________________________________________
5. The Weight of Wear (Soft Interpretation of GSM)
Do NOT present charts.
Instead:
•	Explain weight as “how clothing sits on the body” 
•	Light vs heavy described emotionally: 
o	light = air, summer movement 
o	heavy = structure, grounding 
UI:
•	Three lifestyle silhouettes: 
o	Air weight 
o	Everyday weight 
o	Structured weight (QUTB equivalent feel, but not named as GSM) 
No numbers, no technical metrics.
________________________________________

•	How fabric holds color over time

•	Muted gradient palettes

________________________________________

Focus:
•	Oversized silhouettes

•	Editorial lookbook grid
